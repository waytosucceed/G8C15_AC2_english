Coordinating Conjunctions and Correlative Conjunctions


Grammar overview:

Today we mainly focussed on the following:

  • Coordinating conjunctions and their following types: Cumulative, Adversative, Alternative and Ilative
  • Correlative Conjunctions
  • FANBOYS is the acronym for easy remembrance of Coordinating conjunctions
  • Punctuation rules for using coordinating and correlative conjunctions respectively.

Types of Conjunctions Examples
1. Cumulative Coordinating Conjunctions And, Also, As well as
2. Adversative Coordinating Conjunctions But and Yet
3. Alternative Coordinating Conjunctions Or, Nor, Either, Neither, Else, Otherwise
4. Illative Coordinating Conjunctions For and so
5. Correlative Conjunctions Either-or, Neither-nor, Both-and, Whether-or, Not only-But also, As-As, Not-but, Such-that, As many-As, Scarcely-when, Rather-than, No sooner-than


Word meaning:

Bafflement: a feeling of being completely confused and unable to understand.
Use: The captain’s bafflement was alarming.

Deceitfulness: the fact of being dishonest by telling lies and making somebody believe things that are not true
Use: You will be a loser if you try to win the world by deceitfulness.

Depravity: the state of being morally bad or being wicked
Use: The government’s depravity led to its downfall
